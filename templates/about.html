About Page

The formation of the group grew out of a meeting Gandalf had with Thorin in Bree which kindled Thorin's interest in recapturing his long lost family inheritance. (Thorin's grandfather had been the king of the Lonely Mountain when Smaug came and took it.) Remembering that he had once known an adventurous Hobbit on his travels in the Shire, Gandalf decided to add Bilbo to their company because he knew that stealth and cunning were preferable to force. Gandalf also believed that someone like Bilbo could keep the sometimes prideful and stubborn Dwarves from rash action. The superstitious Dwarves also considered thirteen to be an unlucky number, and as Gandalf had planned to leave on other business, welcomed a fourteenth to fill into their party.

Thorin Oakenshield

In the year TA 2746, Thorin II was born to Dwarven prince Thráin II in the mountain city of The Lonely Mountain. Early in his youth, he and the other Dwarves of the Lonely Mountain were forced to flee by the dragon Smaug in TA 2770. While in exile, he quickly grew to become a capable warrior. This was demonstrated when he participated in the Battle of Azanulbizar in TA 2799. He marched with one of the Dwarven armies beneath the East-gate of Moria. At some point in the fighting, his shield broke, and using an oaken tree branch found on the floor as a shield, he gained the epithet "Oakenshield," which would remain with him even in death.

Fili and Kili

Fíli and Kíli were nephews of Thorin, who was the elder brother of their mother Dís. They were also nephews of Frerin, grandsons of Thráin II, and great-grandsons of Thrór. Fíli's most distinguishing feature was his long nose, the longest of any of the Dwarves in Thorin's company.

Óin

Óin, born in TA 2774, was a member of Thorin's company and the elder brother of Glóin. Known for his skill in fire-making, Óin was a distant cousin of Thorin. He accompanied the company on the quest to reclaim Erebor and played a role in scouting and providing counsel to Thorin.

Gloin

Glóin, born in TA 2783, was a skilled warrior and the father of Gimli, one of the members of the Fellowship of the Ring. Glóin was known for his fiery temper and was one of Thorin's staunch supporters. He played a key role in the Battle of Five Armies and later attended the Council of Elrond in Rivendell.

Balin

Balin, son of Fundin, was born in TA 2763 and was one of Thorin's closest companions. Known for his wisdom and leadership, Balin played a significant role in the quest for Erebor. After the success of the quest, he later led an expedition to reclaim Moria but unfortunately met his end there.

Dwalin

Dwalin, born in TA 2772, was Balin's younger brother. A formidable warrior, Dwalin was known for his loyalty and bravery. He played a crucial role in the Battle of Five Armies and was known for his distinctive blue beard and tattoos.

Ori

Ori, born in TA 2870, was one of the youngest members of Thorin's company. Known for his skill in writing and chronicling, Ori documented much of the journey to Erebor. He met his fate in Moria alongside Balin during their ill-fated attempt to reclaim the ancient Dwarven kingdom.

Dori

Dori, born in TA 2760, was known for his strength and his role as the protector of the company. He often looked out for Bilbo during their journey and was instrumental in saving him from several dangerous situations. Dori was also known for his deep loyalty to Thorin.

Nori

Nori, born in TA 2760, was Dori's brother and a member of Thorin's company. Known for his skills as a thief and his cunning nature, Nori played a significant role in scouting and gathering information during the quest to reclaim Erebor.

Bifur

Bifur, born in TA 2764, was known for the axe embedded in his forehead, a remnant of an old battle injury. Despite this, he was a capable warrior and played an important role in the quest for Erebor. Bifur's resilience and determination were admired by his companions.

Bofur

Bofur, born in TA 2764, was Bifur's cousin and known for his cheerful and optimistic nature. He was a talented toymaker and often lifted the spirits of the company with his humor. Bofur was also brave and loyal, standing by Thorin's side throughout their journey.

Bombur

Bombur, born in TA 2763, was the cousin of Bofur and Bifur. He was the heaviest and most rotund member of Thorin's company, often struggling with physical challenges due to his size. Despite this, Bombur's resilience and good nature made him a valued member of the group.

Gandalf

Gandalf, also known as Mithrandir, was a Maia sent by the Valar to aid the peoples of Middle-earth. Known for his wisdom, power, and kindness, Gandalf played a crucial role in the quest to reclaim Erebor by bringing Bilbo Baggins into Thorin's company. His guidance and interventions were instrumental in the success of their mission.
